AI Governance and Compliance encompasses the frameworks, legal compliance protocols, internal policies, and risk management strategies regulating the ethical design, deployment, and auditing of Artificial Intelligence systems. As AI systems evolve from static models to autonomous, goal-oriented agentic workflows, governance becomes a multi-faceted operational necessity. It ensures that systems comply with international, regional, and national legislative mandates while mitigating security, privacy, and systemic operational risks—such as model hallucination, uncontrolled feedback loops, and runaway token costs.

In an enterprise setting, AI governance addresses the natural tensions between technical capabilities and legal boundaries. Key legislative pillars include the **European Union Artificial Intelligence Act (EU AI Act)**, which implements a risk-based classification system for AI applications (unacceptable, high risk, limited risk, and minimal risk), and personal data frameworks such as the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**, **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)**, and India's **Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act)**. Operationalizing AI governance requires transitioning from reactive compliance tracking to proactive technical guardrails, comprehensive observability layers, and rigorous human-in-the-loop validation checkpoints.

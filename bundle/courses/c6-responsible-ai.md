The **Responsible AI** course (C6) is designed for senior leaders and practitioners to navigate the ethical, organizational, and technological challenges of deploying artificial intelligence in production. Moving beyond theoretical ethics and compliance rules (such as GDPR), the course introduces a pragmatic, leader-oriented approach to ensuring project success. At its core, the curriculum is structured around the proprietary **Darwin Framework**—spanning Data (D), Algorithms (A), Responsibility (R), Workflow Integration (W), and Infrastructure & Security (I)—to address why up to 90% of AI initiatives fail during the prototype or productization phases.

Throughout the course, students explore technical foundations including Large Language Model (LLM) inner workings, [Explainable AI (XAI)](../concepts/explainable-ai.md) techniques, and the transition from basic model prediction to [Agentic AI and Autonomous Systems](../concepts/agentic-ai-autonomous-systems.md). Special emphasis is placed on [AI Governance and Compliance](../concepts/ai-governance-compliance.md) and [AI Lifecycle and MLOps](../concepts/ai-lifecycle-and-mlops.md) as foundational structures. Practitioners analyze real-world case studies, such as the Klarna customer care transformation and competitive intelligence parsing, to understand how [Human-AI Collaboration and Guardrails](../concepts/human-ai-collaboration-guardrails.md) must be integrated to mitigate [Bias, Fairness, and Alignment](../concepts/bias-fairness-alignment.md) issues. In addition, the course covers the engineering of [AI Security and Robustness](../concepts/ai-security-robustness.md) along with model compression techniques for resource-constrained environments.

# Sessions

- [Session 01: Under the Hood of LLMs, the Darwin Framework, and Project Economics](../sessions/c6-responsible-ai-session-01.md) (2026-06-06) — Explores how LLMs are pre-trained, tokenized, and aligned using Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), introducing the Darwin Framework and the economics of project selection.
- [Session 02: Advanced LLM Techniques, Model Shrinking, and Practical Deployment Challenges](../sessions/c6-responsible-ai-session-02.md) (2026-06-07) — Covers Reinforcement Learning (RLHF/RLAIF), DeepSeek's reasoning model innovations, tool calling via the Model Context Protocol (MCP), RAG vs. Fine-Tuning, model shrinking techniques, and real-world deployment resistance.
- [Session 03: A = Algorithm — Product Ideation & Prototyping](../sessions/c6-responsible-ai-session-03.md) (2026-06-21) — Opens the Darwin "A = Algorithm" pillar: why AI initiatives stall, what makes a good prototype, time-boxing builds to days in the AI era, and a structured Translation-Ideation method for turning a business problem into a testable prototype.

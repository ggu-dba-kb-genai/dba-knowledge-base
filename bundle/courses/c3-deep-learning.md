The **Deep Learning** course (C3) is a comprehensive program designed to transition students from foundational mathematical models of artificial neural networks to state-of-the-art deep topologies. This course bridges the gap between classic statistical machine learning and deep representation learning. Students are guided through the theoretical constructs operating under the hood of neural networks—demystifying backpropagation, optimization routines, and complex network architectures—while exploring practical applications across diverse fields including computer vision, natural language processing, and sequence modeling.

Throughout the course, students build a rigorous understanding of feedforward networks, learn how to troubleshoot common training challenges like vanishing gradients and overfitting, and discover how spatial and temporal features are extracted by specialized layers. Moving beyond simple shallow models, the curriculum covers multi-layer architectures, advanced optimizers, spatial convolutions, recurrent state networks, and generative modeling paradigms. This sequence equips students with both the theoretical depth and the practical intuition necessary to design, validate, and optimize neural networks for high-dimensional, unstructured real-world datasets.

# Sessions

- [Session 01: Introduction to Neural Networks & Perceptrons](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-01.md) (2025-11-15) — Explores the biological roots of neural systems, the mathematical formulation of a single perceptron, and the role of transfer and activation functions.
- [Session 02: Introduction to Multi-Layer Perceptrons & Optimization Foundations](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-02.md) (2025-11-16) — Introduces [Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs)](../concepts/neural-network-architectures.md), linear and logistic regression in neural perspective, optimization using convex cost functions, and the fundamentals of gradient descent and maximum likelihood estimation.
- [Session 03: Neural Network Mathematics & Backpropagation Mechanics](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-03.md) (2025-11-22) — Demystifies backpropagation mechanics, matrix and vector notations, the calculus chain rule for layered gradients, and optimization variants like batch and mini-batch gradient descent.
- [Session 04: Overcoming Vanishing Gradients & Activation Functions](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-04.md) (2025-11-23) — Investigates the vanishing gradient problem inherent in deep networks, the mathematical limitations of sigmoid activations in hidden layers, and the introduction of the ReLU family.
- [Session 05: Advanced Optimizers & Hyperparameter Tuning](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-05.md) (2025-11-29) — Examines advanced optimizers (including Adam, RMSprop, and momentum methods) along with learning rate schedules and search spaces for hyperparameter tuning.
- [Session 06: Overfitting, Regularization & Model Validation](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-06.md) (2025-11-30) — Focuses on [Model Evaluation and Validation](../concepts/model-evaluation-validation.md) alongside regularization strategies such as dropout, weight decay, and early stopping to manage the [Bias-Variance Tradeoff](../concepts/bias-variance-tradeoff.md).
- [Session 07: Introduction to Computer Vision & CNNs](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-07.md) (2025-12-06) — Introduces the core challenges of [Computer Vision](../concepts/computer-vision.md), raw image structures, and spatial feature extraction using convolutional kernels.
- [Session 08: CNN Architectures, Pooling & Spatial Representations](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-08.md) (2025-12-07) — Details hierarchical [Neural Network Architectures](../concepts/neural-network-architectures.md), pooling layers, spatial feature mapping, and classic convolutional network designs.
- [Session 09: Transfer Learning & Fine-Tuning in Computer Vision](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-09.md) (2025-12-13) — Explores adaptation strategies for deep pretrained models, exploring task-specific [Transfer Learning and Fine-Tuning](../concepts/transfer-learning-fine-tuning.md) protocols.
- [Session 10: Sequence Modeling, RNNs & LSTMs](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-10.md) (2025-12-14) — Focuses on temporal sequence modeling and [Time Series and Forecasting](../concepts/time-series-forecasting.md) using Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) cells.
- [Session 11: Attention Mechanisms & Sequence-to-Sequence Models](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-11.md) (2025-12-20) — Explores encoder-decoder frameworks, spatial bottlenecks in recurrent loops, and the emergence of [Attention Mechanisms and Transformers](../concepts/attention-and-transformers.md).
- [Session 12: Generative Modeling, Autoencoders & GANs](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-12.md) (2025-12-21) — Covers [Generative Modeling](../concepts/generative-modeling.md) paradigms, including autoencoders (AEs), Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs).
- [Session 13: Model Compression, Optimization & Deployment](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-13.md) (2026-01-04) — Covers model compression techniques such as quantization, pruning, and distillation under [Model Compression and Optimization](../concepts/model-compression-optimization.md).
- [Session 14: Neuromorphic Computing & Hardware Acceleration](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-14.md) (2026-01-10) — Examines spiking neural networks and specialized acceleration architectures under [Neuromorphic and Hardware Acceleration](../concepts/neuromorphic-and-hardware-acceleration.md).
- [Session 15: Course Wrap-up, Lab Review & Advanced Horizons](../sessions/c3-deep-learning-session-15.md) (2026-01-11) — Summarizes core course learnings, walks through implementation labs, and reviews future research horizons in advanced deep architectures.

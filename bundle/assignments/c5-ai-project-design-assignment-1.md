**Course:** AI Project Design & Execution (C5) · **Type:** Individual ·
**Total Marks:** 25 · **Length:** 5 pages (±1)

## Summary

Students critically evaluate **three vendor AI proposals**, each presented as a confident,
polished demo: (1) an **enterprise knowledge assistant** for an energy company, (2) an
**AI-powered HR policy copilot** across geographies, and (3) a **quality-inspection vision
system** for manufacturing. For each, the task is to judge whether the proposed AI approach
is appropriate, separate what is realistic from what is overstated, and decide a suitable
execution path — **Build / Buy / Partner / Platform** — applying concepts from Sessions 1–7
(when to use/not use AI, use-case framing, business risk/cost/governance, and vendor
evaluation). The guidance is to treat each demo as intentionally polished, ask what is
*missing* rather than what looks good, reason on a 3-year (not 3-month) horizon for cost and
lock-in, and be explicit about data, workflow integration, compliance, and failure handling.

## Deliverables

| # | Deliverable | What to include |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A | Demo deconstruction | 6–8 missing questions or hidden assumptions behind the vendor pitch |
| B | Weighted decision matrix | Score Build, Buy, Partner, and Platform on the criteria using a 1–5 scale |
| C | Final recommendation | One recommended path with a short justification |
| D | Risk note | Top 3 risks plus one mitigation each |
| E | Validation before approval | What must be validated before leadership approves the path |

> Scoring scale for the decision matrix: 1 = very weak/unfavorable, 3 = moderate,
> 5 = very strong/favorable. More than one answer can be acceptable if the reasoning is
> strong and trade-offs are explicit. Submit as PDF or Word.

**Course:** Responsible AI (C6) · **Type:** Group (one presentation per group; 10 groups) ·
**Presentation:** 15 minutes (12 present + 3 Q&A)

## Summary

The first Responsible AI assignment puts Session 03's **"A = Algorithm"** ideation method
into practice. Each group selects **one business problem** in any domain and works it
through three linked artifacts — **Ideation, Prototype, and Data** — then presents. Aesthetics
are not graded; depth of content and insight are the focus, and every member should speak at
least once across Assignments 1 and 2.

For the chosen business problem, the team must:

- **Ideation** — define the problem, the prototype, and the hypothesis being tested, using the
  course **Translation-Ideation** worksheet. Sections 1–4 are mandatory; Sections 5–6 are optional.
- **Data** — complete the data document for the problem and identify the **sources of data** to be used.
- **Prototype** — produce a **prototype diagram** and justify (a) why it is a *prototype and not a
  product*, and (b) which **business and technical metrics** will be tested in the prototype.
- **Presentation** — open with the business problem, then summarize the ideation document, the data
  document and diagram, the metrics, and other prototype aspects.

## Translation-Ideation worksheet (deliverable structure)

The mandatory ideation document is structured in six sections (first four required):

1. **Nature of the problem** — categorize the problem and judge whether it needs an AI/DL/GenAI solution at all (some categories indicate a non-AI or automation solution).
2. **End user & expected workflow** — who uses the output and how they act on it.
3. **Business & ROI evaluation** — how the problem is addressed today and the value case.
4. **Data & integration details** — data needed and how it integrates.
5. *(optional)* **Deployment & compliance considerations.**
6. *(optional)* **Priority computation score.**

## Evaluation

Per the course's evaluation criteria, grading is based on the team presentations delivered
during the course. Teams must be formed by the end of the first week; all members receive
equal grades unless it is explicitly communicated that a member did not contribute; and each
member must present in at least one of the two presentation rounds.

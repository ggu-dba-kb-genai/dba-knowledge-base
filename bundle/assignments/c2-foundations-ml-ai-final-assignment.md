**Course:** Foundations of Machine Learning and AI (C2) · **Type:** Group ·
**Report:** ~10–15 pages · **Split:** 70% group / 30% individual

## Summary

Framed for senior leaders, the brief asks each group to build an **"AI/ML Algorithm
Playbook"** for one industry, grounded in the classical algorithms covered in the course
(Linear Regression, Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, KNN, Apriori, K-Means/Medians/
Modes, Hierarchical Clustering, AdaBoost, Random Forests, ARIMA). Each member is the
**primary owner of one distinct algorithm**, identifies a strong real or realistic
industry use case, explains the business problem and data, justifies why the algorithm
fits (data/problem type, interpretability, bias–variance), links it to **business KPIs**,
and describes **evaluation metrics and validation** (confusion matrix, precision/recall,
ROC-AUC, MAPE/RMSE, k-fold CV) plus limitations (over/underfitting, data quality). GenAI
may be used for discovery and outlining, but facts must be verified, sources cited, and
text written by the team.

## Deliverables

- **Group report (~10–15 pages):** executive summary; industry & data landscape; algorithm
  playbook (~1 page per algorithm); cross-algorithm comparison; references (8–10 credible
  sources) and a contribution matrix.
- **Individual reflection note (1–2 pages per student):** the algorithm owned, what was
  learned about applying it in real scenarios, and reflections on evaluation and
  bias–variance; attached as a per-student appendix.

## Rubric

**Group component**

| Criterion | Description | Points |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Quality and relevance of use cases | Use cases real/realistic, clearly described, strongly tied to the industry; credible cited sources. | 20 |
| Correct mapping of problems to algorithms | Understanding of which algorithm fits which problem type (supervised/unsupervised/time-series, classification vs regression). | 10 |
| Treatment of evaluation and metrics | Appropriate use of confusion matrix, accuracy, precision, recall, ROC-AUC, regression error metrics, MAPE/RMSE for ARIMA; correct reasoning. | 10 |
| Bias–Variance, overfitting and model validation | Understanding of bias–variance tradeoff, over/underfitting, k-fold CV or train/test/validation splits, monitoring/drift. | 10 |
| Cross-algorithm insight | Clear comparative insights and tradeoffs in interpretability, performance, and business fit. | 10 |
| Clarity, structure and executive communication | Well-structured, concise yet rich; compelling, visually clear, executive-appropriate. | 10 |

**Individual component**

| Criterion | Description | Points |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Quality of individual sections | Conceptual accuracy, depth, and clarity of the owned algorithm (report body + appendix). | 15 |
| Individual reflection note | Thoughtfulness, evidence of learning, linkage to own context/organization. | 15 |

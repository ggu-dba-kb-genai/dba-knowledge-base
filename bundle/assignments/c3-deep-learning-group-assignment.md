**Course:** Deep Learning & its Variants (C3) · **Type:** Group ·
**Total Marks:** 40 · **Format:** PDF, ≤ 2,500 words

## Summary

Each group picks **one** deep learning technique or architectural paradigm and traces its
**complete evolutionary journey** — from inception to current state-of-the-art — focusing
on how research-driven innovations evolved, what technical limitations motivated new
architectures, and how those advances translated into real-world systems with measurable
business impact. Suggested topics include Image Generation, Image Super-Resolution,
Text-to-Image, Text-to-Speech / Speech-to-Text, Image-to-Image Translation, Text
Generation / Language Modeling, Machine Translation, Object Detection, and Semantic
Segmentation (or a self-chosen problem). The emphasis is **conceptual understanding,
evolution, and impact** — not coding or implementation.

## Required sections

1. **Historical context & problem motivation** — the original problem and limitations of earlier approaches; key research milestones and breakthroughs.
2. **Technical evolution & architectural progression** — a timeline of architectural evolution; how each method addressed prior limitations; major conceptual shifts and design philosophies.
3. **Current state of the art** — the most recent architectures and models; current performance benchmarks and capabilities.
4. **Real-world application & business impact** — select one specific application with clear industry impact; explain how the technique's evolution enabled it; discuss industry adoption (companies, products, platforms), value creation (cost reduction, revenue, scalability, automation), measurable business impact (efficiency/accuracy gains, market disruption, new capabilities), and societal implications where relevant.

> Each group must choose exactly one topic; focus on evolution and impact rather than
> implementation. (Example mappings: Image Generation → marketing creatives; Object
> Detection → autonomous driving or retail analytics; Machine Translation → cross-border
> e-commerce and localization.)

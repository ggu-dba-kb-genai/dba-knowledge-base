**Course:** Emerging Digital Technologies (C1) · **Type:** Group (3–5 members) ·
**Length:** 3,000–3,500 word report + 10–15 minute presentation, APA format ·
**Weight:** 50 marks (50% of overall grade)

## Summary

Student groups conceptualize and present a comprehensive **framework for applying one or
more emerging technologies** (IoT, quantum, neuromorphic computing, RPA, blockchain,
digital twins, etc.) within a chosen industry; teams may extend their individual
Assignment 1. The project must address a **real business challenge**, propose a
**technology-integration strategy**, describe the nature of the transformation and how the
technologies are deployed, cover **strategic rationale and organizational impact**,
**risks/challenges and ethical & governance considerations**, a **cost-benefit / ROI
evaluation**, a **visual framework or architecture**, and close with clear **business
recommendations**. The first presentation slide must state each member's individual
contribution, and all members must attend the presentation.

## Deliverables

- Framework report (3,000–3,500 words, APA), submitted as a single PDF per group.
- Group presentation (10–15 minutes) with a contribution slide (name / ID / contribution).

## Rubric (50 marks)

| Criteria | Excellent | Good | Fair | Poor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Problem Definition (10) | Realistic, high-impact challenge; clearly articulated (9–10) | Clear challenge but may lack industry relevance (7–8) | Generic or shallow (5–6) | Vague or not aligned with business (0–4) |
| Framework Design & Technology Fit (10) | Innovative, well-structured, tech fit clearly explained (9–10) | Good fit, some gaps in technical depth (7–8) | High-level or generic design (5–6) | No clear framework or misaligned tech (0–4) |
| Ethical & Strategic Considerations (10) | Covers ethical, regulatory, strategic alignment (9–10) | Touches on ethics and business logic (7–8) | Partial or unclear coverage (5–6) | Ignored or misunderstood (0–4) |
| ROI / Value Proposition (5) | Strong quantitative and qualitative ROI case (4–5) | ROI estimated but limited justification (3–4) | Weak or unclear value (2–3) | No ROI or benefit discussed (0–2) |
| Visuals / Architecture (5) | High-quality framework diagrams, clear flow (4–5) | Mostly clear visuals (3–4) | Cluttered or inconsistent (2–3) | Missing or confusing (0–2) |
| Presentation Delivery (5) | Engaging, well-coordinated, insightful Q&A (4–5) | Clear delivery, minor timing/flow issues (3–4) | Disjointed or monotonous (2–3) | Poor flow, unreadable slides (0–2) |
| Teamwork & Balance (5) | All members contribute equally (4–5) | Minor imbalance (3–4) | Unequal participation (2–3) | Dominated by one or two members (0–2) |

## Overview

This group assignment requires teams to select a real-world business problem in any domain and design an AI-driven solution. The focus is on the early stages of the AI lifecycle: ideation, prototyping, and data sourcing.

## Core Deliverables

1. **Ideation Document**: Teams must complete Sections 1 through 4 (mandatory) and may optionally complete Sections 5 and 6. This document defines the business problem, the proposed prototype, and the core hypotheses to be tested.
2. **Data Document**: A comprehensive document detailing the required data sources, types, and acquisition strategies for the proposed solution.
3. **Prototype Diagram & Justification**: A visual representation of the prototype architecture. Teams must explicitly justify:
   - Why the current design is classified as a prototype rather than a fully realized product.
   - The specific business and technical metrics that will be used to evaluate the prototype's performance.

## Presentation Guidelines

- **Format**: A 15-minute group presentation (12 minutes for the pitch, 3 minutes for Q&A).
- **Content**: Presentations must cover the business problem, summaries of the ideation and data documents, the prototype diagram, and the evaluation metrics.
- **Evaluation Focus**: Grading prioritizes the depth of insights and content quality over visual aesthetics. All group members must participate in speaking roles across the course's first two assignments.

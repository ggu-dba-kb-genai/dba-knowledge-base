This session serves as the comprehensive orientation for the Doctor of Business Administration (DBA) in Technology program. Led by Dr. Sumitra Padman, the session establishes the overall academic roadmap, outlines the core differences between a DBA (applied, business-centric research) and a PhD (theory-generating research), and introduces the curriculum for the first course, [Emerging Digital Technologies](../courses/c1-emerging-digital-technologies.md). 

Dr. Sumitra outlines the 3-year doctoral journey, which consists of 14 months of coursework followed by a 22-month dissertation phase. She presents the program's structural pillars—Strategic Application, Scalable Implementation, and Model Selection—while guiding candidates on how to formulate applied research problems from their industry experiences. She shares her personal research background (collaborating with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) on machine learning models for space weather forecasting) to illustrate how core technical methodologies apply to complex prediction and decision systems.

The second half of the session previews the modules and faculty of the Emerging Digital Technologies course. Candidates engage in an interactive Q&A exploring how to scope research topics, overcome corporate data sharing constraints using synthetic datasets or simulations, and validate advanced frameworks (such as digital twins or automation pipelines) qualitatively through expert feedback.

# Key Concepts

* **Applied Doctoral Research** — The GGU DBA program prioritizes applied research that bridges theoretical foundations with real-world business, administrative, or social implications, contrasting with traditional PhD programs that prioritize pure theory generation.
* **Dissertation Pathways** — Candidates can complete their dissertation in one of three formats, all maintaining identical academic and research rigor:
    * **Traditional Thesis** — Recommended for deep-dives into a specific conceptual or empirical topic.
    * **Book** — Well-suited for senior leaders proposing strategic frameworks or examining macro-industry technology gaps.
    * **Business Plan** — Ideal for candidates with entrepreneurial objectives who have access to market research data.
* **Research Scoping & Qualifying Milestones** — The dissertation phase is structured into three clear milestones over 22 months:
    1. **Topic Proposal (Qualifying Exam)** — Submission of the first two chapters (Introduction and Literature Review) to serve as a qualifying hurdle.
    2. **Research Proposal Defense** — Presentation of research design and methodology (Chapter 3).
    3. **Final Dissertation Defense** — Presentation of findings, analysis, and conclusions.
* **Data Scarcity & Validation Strategies** — Addressing corporate privacy constraints, the program supports the use of synthetic datasets, public databases (such as government portals), or simulation tools (e.g., Excel or dedicated simulators) to demonstrate technical feasibility. For advanced or proprietary technologies, qualitative validation via structured expert interviews is an accepted alternative.
* **[Time Series and Forecasting](../concepts/time-series-forecasting.md)** — Dr. Sumitra Padman's research background with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) utilized regression and deep learning time series algorithms to forecast space weather parameters.
* **[Bias-Variance Tradeoff](../concepts/bias-variance-tradeoff.md)** — Highlighted during the preview of the machine learning module, where understanding concepts like overfitting and underfitting is crucial to ensuring that business decision-making models remain robust and generalizable.
* **[Neuromorphic and Hardware Acceleration](../concepts/neuromorphic-and-hardware-acceleration.md)** — Edge computing hardware designed to mimic biological neural structures, optimizing the tradeoff between computational performance and power efficiency for processing IoT sensor data.
* **[Decentralized AI and Blockchain](../concepts/decentralized-ai-and-blockchain.md)** — Investigates secure distributed ledgers, smart contracts, and decentralized data validation for enterprise security and multi-party computation.
* **[AI Security and Robustness](../concepts/ai-security-robustness.md)** — Previewed under cloud, cyber, and model security contexts to address the critical trust, adversarial defense, and data protection issues organizations face during AI integrations.
* **[AI Governance and Compliance](../concepts/ai-governance-compliance.md)** — Outlined as a key area of study focused on managing organizational risk, regulatory compliance, change management, and responsible AI implementation frameworks.
* **[Agentic AI and Autonomous Systems](../concepts/agentic-ai-autonomous-systems.md)** — Focuses on multi-agent collaboration, goal-driven actions, and decision-making feedback loops, highlighting the shift from static automation to intelligent, autonomous agent frameworks.

* **Instructor Background & Academic Journey** — Dr. Sumitra Padman’s transition from electronic hardware, process control, and space weather forecasting (using regression and time series deep learning at ISRO) to leading academic verticals for DBA tech programs at upGrad.
* **GGU DBA Learning Outcomes** — Overview of scholarly standards, critical analytical thinking, understanding of seminal theories, and ethical/privacy compliance.
* **The 14-Month Coursework Roadmap** — Overview of the six prerequisite courses:
    1. [Emerging Digital Technologies](../courses/c1-emerging-digital-technologies.md)
    2. [Foundations ML AI](../courses/c2-foundations-ml-ai.md)

This session of the **Emerging Digital Technologies (C1)** course serves as a critical milestone in the Doctor of Business Administration (DBA) program. Designed as an interactive forum, the session is dedicated to student group project presentations, peer reviews, and a comprehensive course assessment debrief. Students showcase their ability to translate complex technical advancements into viable strategic business cases, bridging the gap between engineering-level breakthroughs and boardroom-level decision-making.

Throughout the session, student cohorts present their final group projects, demonstrating how a spectrum of emerging technologies can be integrated to solve actual corporate challenges or doctoral research problems first identified in [Session 01](c1-emerging-digital-technologies-session-01.md). These group presentations require students to defend their architectural choices—such as selecting between edge and cloud compute, evaluating the business case for [Decentralized AI and Blockchain](../concepts/decentralized-ai-and-blockchain.md), or incorporating [Model Compression and Optimization](../concepts/model-compression-optimization.md) for local enterprise deployment.

The session concludes with a detailed course assessment debrief. This debrief traces the academic arc of the entire course, evaluating how the student body has synthesized topics ranging from edge sensors and Internet of Things (IoT) protocols to advanced concepts like [Neuromorphic and Hardware Acceleration](../concepts/neuromorphic-and-hardware-acceleration.md), spatial computing, and robotic process automation (RPA). This review solidifies foundational knowledge and prepares students for their scholarly research and upcoming advanced coursework in machine learning and artificial intelligence.

# Key Concepts

- **Applied Emerging Technologies Showcase** — Student-led presentations demonstrating the strategic corporate integration of advanced digital technologies to address complex organizational inefficiencies or market opportunities.
- **DBA Research Problem Integration** — Mapping the digital frameworks explored throughout the course back to the core research methodologies and organizational problem-solving frameworks established in [Session 01](c1-emerging-digital-technologies-session-01.md).
- **Course Assessment and Debrief** — A systemic synthesis of the C1 curriculum, reviewing how emerging technologies serve as key building blocks for subsequent courses like [Foundations ML AI](../courses/c2-foundations-ml-ai.md).
- **Technical Architecture Defensibility** — The process of evaluating and justifying specific hardware and software integration paths, such as [AI Security and Robustness](../concepts/ai-security-robustness.md) and [Agentic AI and Autonomous Systems](../concepts/agentic-ai-autonomous-systems.md), in enterprise settings.

# Topics Covered

- **Student Group Project Presentations**: Deliverables demonstrating technical architectures, implementation timelines, risk analysis, and strategic business values for selected emerging technologies.
- **Peer Evaluation & Q&A Defenses**: Structured feedback sessions where presenting teams defend their technological selections, security mitigations, and scalability plans.
- **Curriculum Debrief & Key Learnings**: A high-level retrospective of the 13 preceding instructional sessions, emphasizing the cohesive path from data gathering at the physical edge to cloud services and decentralized ledgers.
- **Research Path Alignment**: Guidance on how to transition corporate-facing technology projects into rigorous, publishable doctoral-level research contributions.
